Key Words and Concept:

  1. -ian/ean: a suffix that makes adjectives, for example "Asian"

  2. -ly: a suffix that makes adverbs, for example "carefully"

  3. -self: a suffix that makes reflexive pronouns, for example "herself"

  4. -y: a suffix that makes adjectives, for example "salty"

  5. -able: a suffix that makes adjectives, for example "controllable"

  6. -er: a suffix that makes comparative adjectives, for example, "hotter"

Introduction

The study of words, in addition to helping a person increase his vocabulary, can be extremely interesting and even fun. Below, some useful suffixes are introduced. If you study these word parts carefully, you will increase your knowledge of words. In your reading also pay attention to the structure of words whose meanings you do not know.

 

-ian/ean: born in or living in; believing in

England and France are in Europe. They are European Countries.

 

-ly:   in the manner of; this word tells how

John is a quick runner. He runs very quickly.

 

-self:   referring to the same person or thing

Ali was cutting the apple with a knife. Ali cut himself.

 

-y: (adjective) the existence or condition of

There is a lot of wind in the Spring here. It is very windy in the Spring here.

 

-able: (adjective) capable of

The chair gives you comfort. It is a comfortable chair.

 

-er: (adjective) more

Reza is tall, but Amin is very tall. In other words, Amin is taller than Reza.
