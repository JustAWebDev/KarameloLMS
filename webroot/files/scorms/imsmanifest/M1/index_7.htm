Word Recognition and chunking

 

Key Word Objects:

  • Instructional Objectives

  • Word Recognition

  • Sentence Chunking

  • Multiple-choice Items

Key Words and Concept:

  1. Chunk: divide into smaller parts

  2. Eye span: the width your eyes can see

  3. Glance: a short look

  4. Recognition: The ability to know what something is
 

 Instructional Objectives:

1. Increasing your reading speed

2. Expanding your eye span

3. Knowing how to divide sentences into meaningful groups of words

UP
 

Word Recognition

Try to look at a group of three words in each glance and quickly circle the words which are the same as the first one in each line. Each line will be displayed for 3 seconds. This exercise helps you to expand your eye span.

Sentence Chunking

Read the following passage in word groups as separated by slashes. Fix your eyes at the center of each group and read it as one piece

 

As Tom/ was on his way/ to school that morning/ he met/ Huckleberry Finn./ Huck/ was the son/ of the town drunkard./ He was disliked/ by all the mothers/ of the town/ because/ he was lazy,/ lawless,/ rude and bad./ None of the children/ were allowed/ to play with him./ Tom was/ like the rest,/ and wished/ he could live/ like Huck,/ but he was/ under orders/ not to play/ with him./ So/ he played with him/ every time/ he got the chance./

Huckleberry/ was always dressed/ in old clothes/ that were/ far too big/ for him./ He wore/ a dirty hat/ and nothing on his feet./ He slept/ on doorsteps/ in fine weather,/ and in an empty barrel/ when it was wet./  He did not have to go/ to school/ or to church,/ and he/ did not have to/  obey anybody./ He could go/ fishing or swimming/ when he liked./  Nobody told him/ not to fight,/ and he could/ go to bed/ when he wished./ He never had to/ wash or put on/ clean clothes./ He was/ every boy's dream/ of what a boy should be.

Multiple-choice Items

 Answer the following items by choosing the best choice.

1. Which of the following sentences is chunked in the best way?

 a. No one knows/ all the reasons/ why languages change,/ but they continue/ to do so/ as long as/ people speak them./

 

 b. No one/ knows all/ the reasons/ why languages/ change, but/ they continue/ to do so as/ long as/ people speak them./
c. No one/ knows all the/ reasons why/ languages change,/ but they/ continue to do/ so as/ long as people/ speak them./

 

 d. No/ one knows/ all the reasons/ why languages/ change, but/ they continue to/ do so as/ long as people/ speak them./

 

2. Choose the sentence that has been chunked correctly.

 

 a.  A number is/ an idea that/ is used to/ refer to amounts/ of things./ People use/ number words,/ number gestures,/ and number symbols./

 

 b. A number is an/ idea that is/ used to/ refer to amounts of/ things./ People/ use number/ words,/ number gestures,/ and number symbols./
c. A number is/ an idea that is/ used to refer to amounts of things./ People/ use number/ words, number gestures,/ and number symbols./

d. A number/ is an idea/ that is used/ to refer/ to amounts of things./ People use/ number words,/ number gestures,/ and number symbols./
